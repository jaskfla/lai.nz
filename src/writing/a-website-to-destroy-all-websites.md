> We’re down from a thousand and one websites to three, and each of those
> remaining monolith websites is just a soullessly-regurgitated,
> compression-down-scaled,
> <abbr title="artificial intelligence">AI</abbr>-up-scaled version of the next.
>
> …now those social networks, once hand-built and hand-tended, vibrant and
> organic, are unceremoniously swallowed by social _networks_, pens built for
> trapping us and our little piggy attentions, turning us all into clout-chasers
> & content-creators, and removing us from what meaningful intimacy & community
> felt like.

There’s knowing your way around written words, and there’s knowing your way
around the written word. What a superlative demonstration of the latter.

Henry’s essay is at once an exquisite articulation of what I—clearly not
alone—feel in my bones, and an exemplary website that stakes its claim
(reclamation?) to the web.
