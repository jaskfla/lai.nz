Kia ora e hoa! Jasper here. I do stuff with the web. I’m not currently open for new work; but here’s a li’l something about how I approach my work and my workplace.

A bit about me

My jobby-job calls me a full-stack web developer. Really, I’m a design engineer. The front-of-the-front-end sort, for whom design and engineering are different perspectives of the same thing.

I love web technologies, and I love the web. JavaScript has a lot of stupid in it, and CSS has some tech debt. But that hasn’t stopped people from making some pretty cool shit. It also isn’t governed by a single, for-profit company—and that’s rather neat.

I’m attuned to the web’s principles, and work with its grain. I keep up-to-date with draft, spec and browser updates. I have annoying opinions about the cascade, Locality of Behaviour and you-name-it. But my interfaces are adaptive, my markup debuggable, and my work inclusive. I code with semantics, progressive enhancement and design research.

Also, I’m a big font nerd. Real big.

A bit about you

Your work and your process are design-led, informed by your ethical values.

Your consensus are borne by your alignment, not vice versa.

Your team feel ownership over their work. They are afforded the agency to do the thing and do the thing well.

You value families, but aren’t one.

You see the world through a lens of relationships, not a series of discrete projects.

You have collaborators, not individual contributors.

Your teams are small. Humans work best at human-scale.

Proficiencies & idiosyncrasies

This is not a SWOT, merely an articulation of characteristics. Some may present unambiguously as strengths, others as clear weaknesses. All can be framed as either; I’ll let you judge.

I’ve been described as focused, questioning and attentive to momentum.

React is my bread-and-butter, though I’m happiest when I don’t need it.

I have an appetite for resilience, for inclusion, and for better—none of which are easily measurable.

I know my way around Figma, am fluent in HTML and CSS, and understand the intricacies of design systems.

I’m an unremarkable ideator but a strong editor.

I’m an interior-of-the-cabinet painter. Thorough down to the last detail, or something like that.

I am unironically, unashamedly curious.

Beliefs & ways

Inclusion isn’t a stretch goal.

The web is document-first. HTML, CSS and JavaScript should be used as minimally as possible, and in that order.

Utility-first CSS is antithetical to web sensibilities.

The internet can be fun.

Capitalism isn’t working out.

LLMs are a fascinating tool, but stewards of the technology have built their empire on theft.

Detail matters.
